What Makes This Different

This Is Not Visualization

In scripted visualization, the guide leads. In Interactive Guided Imagery, the client leads. Their own imagery becomes the material — not a script. The practitioner follows with skill and ethical structure.

Images Precede Words

The nervous system processes imagery before verbal thought. Change begins in perception, not explanation.

The Client's World Leads

No scripts. No leading. The practitioner follows what emerges — safely, ethically, skillfully.

40 Years of Structure

Used in hospitals, private practice, and research settings. Built with ethical guardrails from day one.

Grounded in Research

Why Clinicians Take This Seriously

Experiential Processing in Neuroscience

Guided imagery engages experiential and imaginal processing systems studied in neuroscience and embodied cognition — offering practitioners a way to work with material often difficult to access through discussion alone.

Neuroplasticity Supports Lasting Change

Repeated imagery practice has been associated with neural circuit adaptation in rehabilitation and performance research. For practitioners, this means the work can compound over time.

Depth Psychology Provides the Framework

IGI draws on Jungian active imagination and psychosynthesis — working with the symbolic language of the unconscious as a legitimate clinical resource, not a metaphor.
